Create a premium, high-end landing page for a residential roofing contractor.

This is NOT a marketing agency site.
This is NOT a SaaS startup.
This is NOT flashy.

This should look like a $5M+ revenue roofing company with strong local authority.

Company Name:
Apex Roofing & Exteriors

Location:
Dallas, Texas

Target Audience:
Homeowners who need roof replacement, storm damage repair, or insurance claim assistance.

Primary Goal:
Generate inspection calls and estimate requests.

Design Direction:
- Clean
- Modern
- Trustworthy
- Established
- Professional
- Minimal clutter
- Strong spacing
- Structured sections
- High-quality photography
- Masculine but refined
- No trendy startup gradients
- No overuse of animations
- No playful UI
- No tech startup aesthetics

Typography:
- Strong serif or bold sans-serif headline font
- Clean, readable body font
- Clear hierarchy
- Large hero headline
- Spacious line height

Color Palette:
- Deep navy or charcoal primary
- White backgrounds
- Subtle light gray sections
- One strong accent color (roofing red or construction orange)
- High contrast CTA buttons

Layout Structure:

1. HERO SECTION
Full-width hero with strong background image (real residential roof, not abstract)
Overlay gradient for readability.

Headline:
Clear, direct, benefit-driven.

Subheadline:
Reinforce trust and specialization.

Primary CTA button:
“Schedule Free Roof Inspection”

Secondary CTA:
“Get Instant Estimate”

Trust indicators below buttons:
- Licensed & Insured
- 25+ Years Experience
- 5-Star Rated
- Insurance Claim Specialists

Include phone number top right in header.

2. TRUST BAR
Horizontal strip with:
- Google 5-star rating
- Manufacturer certifications (GAF / Owens Corning style badges)
- Warranty badge (Lifetime Workmanship Warranty)
- Fully Licensed & Insured badge

Clean, evenly spaced, subtle background contrast.

3. PROBLEM + SOLUTION SECTION
Two-column layout.

Left:
Headline addressing homeowner pain:
Storm damage, leaks, insurance confusion.

Right:
Clear paragraph explaining:
How Apex Roofing handles everything from inspection to insurance approval to installation.

Keep copy concise.
No marketing fluff.

4. SERVICES SECTION
Grid layout with icons or images.

Services:
- Roof Replacement
- Storm Damage Repair
- Insurance Claim Assistance
- Roof Inspections
- Emergency Repairs

Each service card:
Short headline
1–2 sentence description
“Learn More” link

Clean cards with soft shadow and consistent spacing.

5. PROCESS SECTION
Horizontal 4-step process with clean icons.

Step 1: Free Inspection
Step 2: Insurance Support
Step 3: Professional Installation
Step 4: Final Walkthrough & Warranty

Minimal text.
Strong clarity.

6. BEFORE & AFTER SECTION
High-impact section.
Use image slider comparison style.

Headline:
“See the Difference Quality Makes”

Large imagery.
No clutter.

7. SOCIAL PROOF / TESTIMONIALS
3 clean testimonial cards.
Include:
- Name
- Location
- 5-star indicator
- Short authentic review

No generic praise.
Make it sound realistic.

8. REVENUE & VALUE SECTION (Subtle Authority)
Headline:
“Built to Protect Your Home for Decades”

Explain:
- Premium materials
- Long-term warranty
- Storm resilience
- Increased home value

This section should feel strong and reassuring.

9. FINANCING SECTION
Clean split layout.

Left:
Headline:
“Flexible Financing Available”

Right:
Brief explanation:
Low monthly payments
Fast approval
No large upfront burden

Include “Check Financing Options” button.

10. STRONG FINAL CTA SECTION
Full-width dark background.
Large bold headline.

Example:
“Don’t Wait Until Minor Damage Becomes a Major Expense”

Primary CTA:
Schedule Free Roof Inspection

Phone number large and visible.

11. FOOTER
Professional footer layout.
Include:
- Address (Dallas, TX)
- Phone number
- Email
- Service areas
- Quick links
- Social icons
- License number

Visual Rules:
- Generous white space between sections
- Clear separation of sections using light gray backgrounds
- Buttons large and visible
- No tiny fonts
- No dense paragraphs
- No over-decoration
- Keep it structured and symmetrical

Image Direction:
Use real residential roofing photography.
Use drone shots.
Use installation action shots.
Avoid stock models smiling at camera.
Avoid fake office scenes.

Overall Feel:
When a roofing contractor sees this page, they should think:
“This looks like a serious, established company.”
“This feels trustworthy.”
“This would convert homeowners.”

It must NOT look like:
A marketing template.
A design portfolio.
A startup SaaS product.
An agency website.

It must look like:
A premium local contractor dominating their market.

Focus on clarity, authority, trust, and conversion.
